Solar system mounting Structures

The fabrication of solar structures involves several steps and processes, including designing, material selection, cutting, shaping, and assembly. Here is a general overview of the fabrication process:

  • Designing: The first step in the fabrication of solar structures is designing. This includes creating a 3D model of the structure and determining the material requirements and specifications.
  • Material Selection: Once the design is finalized, the appropriate materials are selected. These materials should be lightweight, durable, and corrosion-resistant, such as aluminum, stainless steel, or galvanized steel.
  • Cutting and Shaping: The selected materials are then cut and shaped into the desired forms using various techniques, such as laser cutting, water jet cutting, or CNC milling. This step requires precision to ensure the parts fit together properly.
  • Welding and Joining: After cutting and shaping, the parts are welded or joined together to form the structure. This can be done using various methods, including TIG welding, MIG welding, or mechanical fasteners.
  • Surface Treatment: Once the structure is formed, it is treated to protect against corrosion and enhance its appearance. This can include anodizing, powder coating, or painting.
  • Assembly: Finally, the solar panels are assembled onto the structure, and the structure is installed onto the site. This includes attaching the panels to the structure using clamps or mounting hardware and securing the structure to the ground or roof.

There are several types of solar structures that are commonly used to mount solar panels and arrays. Here are some of the most common types:

  • Flat Roof Mountings

    This is the most common type of solar structure and is designed to be installed on the roof of a building. It can be attached to the roof using a variety of methods, including bolts, clips, and ballasts.

  • Ground Mountings

    Ground-mounted solar structures are installed on the ground and are typically used when there is not enough space on the roof for the required number of solar panels. They can be fixed or tracking, which means that they follow the movement of the sun throughout the day to maximise energy production.

  • Blasted Mountings

    Blasted mountings are specially designed for industrial applications where strength and durability are paramount. They are ideal for harsh environments.

  • Auto Trackers

    Auto trackers follow the sun's movement to optimize solar energy production throughout the day. They adjust automatically to maximize efficiency.

  • Manual Trackers

    Manual trackers require periodic adjustment to track the sun's movement. They are less expensive than auto trackers but require human intervention.

  • Special Structures

    Special structures are customized solutions for unique installations, such as solar carports, floating solar systems, and more.

  • Car Parking Sheds

    Car parking sheds are dual-purpose structures that provide shelter for vehicles while generating solar energy.

Poles Fabrications

  • Street Light Poles

    Street light poles are fabricated to support street lights and are designed to withstand harsh weather conditions. They can be equipped with additional features such as sensors and cameras.

  • VMS Poles

    Variable Message Sign (VMS) poles are used to mount digital displays for traffic and public information purposes.

  • Camouflage Poles

    Camouflage poles are designed to blend into the environment and are often used in sensitive areas such as nature reserves or military bases.

  • Stadium Light Poles

    Stadium light poles are tall structures that support large lighting systems to illuminate sports arenas and stadiums.

  • Octagonal Poles

    Octagonal poles are strong and durable structures commonly used for street and highway lighting.

  • Conical Poles

    Conical poles have a tapered design that provides both strength and aesthetics. They are often used in urban settings for street lighting.

  • Tubular Poles

    Tubular poles are lightweight and easy to install, making them a popular choice for a variety of applications including street lighting.
